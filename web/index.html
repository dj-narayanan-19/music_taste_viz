What am I looking at?

The Map

A UMAP of my music history. My most-listened songs are positioned in 2D space by audio feature similarity — songs that sound alike sit closer together. Bubble size = play count.

Data

Listening history from Last.fm, audio features from SoundCharts (originally Spotify). Read about what the features mean here. UMAP is a dimensionality reduction technique used to make the 10-feature space viewable in 2D.

Artist Neighborhoods

Songs can be colored by artist or by neighborhood. Neighborhoods group artists whose mean song shares similar sound qualities — think genre-like clusters. The number of neighborhoods is adjustable. Click a neighborhood to highlight it and see its average audio features.

Feature Selection

Toggle which audio features are included in the UMAP calculation to see how each one affects the layout.

Terrain

Projects a topographic map onto the background based on a selected audio feature — high/low elevation = high/low values. Legend in the bottom left.

Hover a point to see song details. Click to highlight its 15 nearest neighbors.
